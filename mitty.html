Knot Yourself Out

It’s winter. I don’t know about you, but for me, winter is cold ear season and I hate it. I hate cold ears. So I wear a lot of hats and headbands. I’m going to share with you my favorite knitted headband. It looks super sweet and is cozy warm.

This will be a one size fits all headband, so go ahead and input your gauge and get the measurements.

Get the Pattern in Your Gauge.

Default pattern is for 4 sts/in and 5 rows/in

Strap in, because we are double knitting this knit!

Cast on 29 stitches.

Odd rows: Knit one. Bring the yarn to the front. Slip one. Bring the yarn to the back. Knit one. Bring the yarn to the front. Slip one. Bring the yarn to the back. Continue in this pattern to the end of the row. You will end on a Knit.

Even Rows: When you finish the first row, turn the work and slip the first stitch. Knit one. Bring the yarn to the front. Slip one. Bring the yarn to the back, just like the last row. Continue until the last stitch in the row. Purl this one. Turn the work, and then slip the first stitch.

Continue in this pattern for 22”. This takes a long time, but it gives you a really cool reversible knit that is 2 layers thick. Perfect for walking the dog while it’s snowing.

Now it's time to bind off. On your last row, knit 2 stitches together down the length of the row. You will be left with 15 stitches. Now we start the bind off row. Fold the band in half so that the beginning row is next to your ending row. Pick up one stitch from the beginning of the work and knit the two stitches together. Do that for the rest of the row. The two ends are now combined. Bind off this row. You should be left with a pretty ugly seam. That’s ok, we’re going to cover it up.

Cast on 9 stitches. Knit in stockinette stitch for 3.5”. Keeping this small piece on the needle, wrap it around the headband piece, the knit side facing in. Use the same bind off method as we used for the headband piece. Flip the small piece around so the knit side is facing up.

To make sure the small piece doesn’t move, thread some of the same colored yarn through a darning needle. Near the bind off-seam stick the needle in under the ribbon piece and thread it through the headband piece. Loop it over one row of the ribbon piece on the opposite side and then thread it back through. Tie it off and your done! Go rock your new double knit headband!
