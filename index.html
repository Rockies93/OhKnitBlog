A Knit About Me:

I first started learning to knit at age 12 with a pair of bamboo skewers, some leftover orange yarn from an elementary school biography project display, and printed out instructions from the internet, but only for the purl stitch. My first project was a potholder, my own design. It was only in stockinette stitch, so it didnâ€™t lay flat, but always rolled up into a tight coil, even after I melted the yarn by ironing it with the iron on the hottest setting. It was a glorious beginning to a craft that I have been working on for 15 years now.

When I was first getting into knitting, my aunt got me a year-long subscription to a knitting magazine. Every month I would get a magazine full of stories, tutorials, and patterns all about knitting. Well, 14 year old me spent hours in those magazines and I learned a lot from them. To this day, I still have a sweater that I knitted using one of the patterns from the fall 2007 issue as well as a binder full of cut out articles on how to cable, the best way to knit a fair-isle pattern (without those pesky trails), how to double knit and other knitting techniques. I hope that this blog will do for you what that knitting magazine did for me all those years ago.
